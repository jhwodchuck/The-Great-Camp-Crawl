Journey

What is it

Journey is an 8-month gap year internship in Montana designed for young adults (ages 18-26) looking to develop leadership skills, invest in community, experience adventure, and mature in their relationship with God.

Adventure

Experience the unknown through outdoor sports. Dare to take a risk. Step away from comfort. Discover new boundaries.

We spend most of our time outdoors learning an array of adventure sports!

Participants learn both technical and interpersonal skills through hands-on practice and life application, including survival skills, relational skills, and certification in Wilderness Advanced First Aid (WAFA).

‍

The adventure trainings include:*
  • Backpacking
  • Rafting
  • Kayaking
  • Canoeing
  • Rescue Training
  • Rock Climbing
  • Skiing
  • Winter Survival
  • Mountaineering
  • Wilderness Advanced First Aid (WAFA) Training & Certification

*varies due to altering conditions

Discipleship

Join in a community aspiring to know others deeply, know ourselves truthfully, and know God profoundly.

Participants live onsite, among fellow Journey participants and camp staff, giving them the opportunity to build deep relationships and experience a community who truly does life together.

You will learn directly from staff who love God and are actively pursuing a deeper relationship with Jesus. This community asks questions, seeks truth, and pursues Christ-likeness in everything.

‍

Leadership

Discover your unique style and ability to lead yourself and others. Practice key skills. Gain confidence. Take responsibility.

Our world needs leaders, motivated by their love for God and love for others, equipped with skills and confidence, to serve.

Journey develops these leaders through self-examination, personal training and experiential learning. Participants will have the opportunity to use their leadership skills by guiding practicums through adventures and practicing discipleship.

Cost Breakdown

Same Complete Program, New Reduced Cost: Bighorn is beyond grateful to announce a significant reduction in the program cost! This adjustment is only possible through the remarkable generosity of our donors.

‍

Total Participant Cost

$10,695 (Originally $14,195)

The tuition for the Journey program is $10,695 total. The tuition cost includes room and board, adventure activities, programmatic transportation, and all trainings and certifications. Tuition is broken into two semesters. The $1000 non-refundable deposit is due upon acceptance into the program and will reserve your spot. After the deposit, the first semester balance of $5,295 is due by August 1st, 2026; the second semester balance of $4,400 is due by January 2nd, 2027.

A work study element is included throughout the program to help participants buy down the true cost. A $3500 credit is given to each student, reducing the price from $14,195 to $10,695. The work study provides essential service and ministry opportunities that align with Bighorn's heart of servant leadership. Sample work study components include: leading in a variety of programmed events, assisting with food service, operations, and ground or facilities work.

Frequently Asked Questions

If you can’t find the answer you’re looking for, feel free to reach out! We're here to help and will get back to you as soon as we can.

Contact Us
Do I need outdoor adventure sports experience?

Nope! We will start with the basics on every adventure sport. Our instructors adapt the course to the level of ability and experience of the individuals.

Do I need to bring my own gear?

Journey provides all of the necessary group adventure gear such as kayaks, tents, skis, and PFDs. You do need to purchase personal gear such as a backpacking pack, clothing, hiking boots and a sleeping system. You are welcome to bring any extra personal gear you would like.

Will I have free time?

Yes! Journey programming (typically) runs 5 out of 7 days of the week with most weekends off. There are a few evening events per week, and many evenings of free time as well. Participants are welcome to spend their free time as they please - on or off campus.

Can I bring my own vehicle?

Absolutely! In fact it is encouraged. During free time, participants can take personal vehicles into town, to camp sites, to church events, or for a scenic drive through the mountains.

What's the food like?

Here at Camp Bighorn, we value quality food to prepare us well for adventure and to keep our taste buds happy. We are known for having restaurant quality meals. The food here is diverse and delicious! (Unless you are backpacking. Then it depends on how good of a cook you are ;)
