Summer Engineering Institute

Discover the world of engineering this summer at Georgia Tech. Our one-week residential program invites 11th and 12th graders to explore the fascinating realm of engineering through hands-on experiments and interactive workshops. Participants will stay overnight on campus. They will enjoy guided tours of our state-of-the-art campus, engage directly with cutting-edge technology, and gain invaluable insights into the admissions process at one of the nation’s leading engineering schools. 

Whether you’re just curious about engineering or firmly planning your future in the field, this program is the perfect gateway to expand your knowledge, meet fellow aspiring engineers, and experience life at Georgia Tech. Join us for an unforgettable week of learning and discovery!

Program Design

Students participate in a multitude of activities to explore engineering and computer science. Here are just a few highlights of what your camper will experience during the week:

  • Participate in hands-on experiments and interactive workshops
  • Learn more about the admissions process at Georgia Tech
  • Meet and interact with Georgia Tech professors, students, and staff
Image

Dates

June 7-15, 2026 (SEI is overnight and participants will stay on campus.)

Fee

SEI is free to participants in 2026. 

Applications

SEI is geared toward rising 11th and 12th-grade students who are interested in engineering, science, math, or technology. 

The Summer Engineering Institute is for Georgia residents only.

Registration for the Summer Engineering Institute is now closed.

TryEngineering Summer Institute

The TryEngineering Summer Institute creates meaningful opportunities for thousands of students to explore and engage with engineering disciplines. In collaboration with the Georgia Institute of Technology College of Engineering, a nine-day, residential summer program designed to inspire and empower the next generation of engineers is being planned.

Thanks to generous support from the Georgia Tech Daniel Guggenheim School of Aerospace Engineering, Georgia Space Grant Consortium, IEEE Technical Activities, and industry partners, thirty high school students from Georgia will be able to participate in this transformative experience at no cost.

Throughout the program, students will participate in hands-on design challenges, interactive field trips, and receive mentorship from engineering professionals all while experiencing living on a college campus and developing their leadership potential. The experience fosters curiosity, strengthens problem-solving skills, and ignites a lasting interest in engineering and technology as potential career paths.

Image
