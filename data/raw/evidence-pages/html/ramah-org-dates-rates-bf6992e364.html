Dates & Rates

A kayitz (summer) at Camp Ramah is a summer filled with magical moments.

For some chanichim (campers) it’s reaching the top of the ropes course for the first time, jumping off the diving board at the breicha (pool), or sitting quietly with friends on the givah (hill) watching one of Ojai’s famous sunsets light up the evening sky. For others, it’s making a masterpiece come to life in omanut (arts and crafts), an exciting round of frisbee golf on Shabbat afternoon, or seeing an entire Torah unrolled end to end for the first time.

CANCELLATION POLICY

All applications have a non-refundable application fee of $300, included in the initial deposit.
January 1, 2026 – April 1, 2026: $1,200 cancellation fee.
After April 1, 2026, we are unable to provide refunds.

FIRST-TIME FAMILY DISCOUNT

We are excited to offer all new families our 2025 rates through February 1, 2026 — a saving of $405 for 4-week campers and $220 for 2-week campers! After this date, tuition will increase to our 2026 pricing.

SIBLING DISCOUNT

$75 off for the second child in the same household, increasing by $25 for each additional child.

CREDIT CARD FEE

For all payments made via credit card, there is a 3% fee attached.
We encourage all families to pay by eCheck to avoid the extra charge.

MEDICATION FEE

We charge a medication fee to partially offset the costs of associated with packing, shipping, inspection and verification, storage, and distribution of all medications.
The cost is $50 for one or two medications and rises by $25 for each additional medication, with a maximum of $125.

INSURANCE

We strongly recommend families purchase insurance in order to safeguard your investment in camp tuition. Several options are available for this service; please click here for more details on the offering from MH Ross. Camp Ramah provides this information as a courtesy only.

SESSION CHANGE FEE

After April 15, 2026, there may be a $100 change fee unless the camper switches from Gesher to a full 4-week session.

Carefully note the session dates you select to ensure your camper(s) can attend camp for the full session. Campers can not come late or leave early for summer school, vacations, or other commitments.

We are aware that in some cases, the end of the Spring semester or the start of the Fall semester conflicts with the camp schedule by a couple of days. We can accommodate families with these conflicts by extending a short window of flexibility for late arrivals or early departures that still allows campers to be with us for all Shabbatot. If this applies to you, please be in touch immediately so that these special arrangements can be made.
