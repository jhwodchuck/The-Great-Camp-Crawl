Grades 6-10

Echo Valley


Wild and Inviting

Echo Valley is a relaxed adventure, Texas river style, for campers who have finished sixth through tenth grades. Our daily activities are uniquely wild and inviting—custom rope swings, music-filled swim times, intense group games, epic dance parties, thrilling high adventure elements (rock climb, cliff rappel, etc.), hilarious and profound nightly Roundups and cabin times. But there is also tons of down time to relax and chat with friends by the river, by the Ranch House, or just about anywhere. Guys go deep with MOG. Girls discover what it means to be Rooted. All of us grow as we get to know others and as we reflect on life, faith, and God.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EDZ0aoWLwyE

At Echo Valley, campers can expect to try new things—everything from rappelling steep limestone bluffs to swimming in natural spring-fed waters to country dancing. They might ride in the back of a pick-up on dirt roads in an unbelievably beautiful part of Texas. They might see more stars than they have ever seen. All along the way, they can expect to learn about God and discover ways to grow in their faith.

Echo Valley hosts around 200 campers each session and the average cabin size is about 14 campers per cabin. The counselors are at the core of the camp experience (with an average of four in each cabin) as they lead their campers through all aspects of camp. We work hard to hire the right people. To have a fun camp, we hire fun people. To help campers deepen their faith, we hire folks deeply committed in their own faith and who are geared toward ministry. To help campers grow and thrive, we find folks with the personal and spiritual maturity to be role models to our campers.

Echo Valley is located on an enormous bend in the Frio River about a mile and a half downstream from Singing Hills, and, yes, you have to drive in the river to get there. All we need is located right on our peninsula—ropes courses, guys’ and girls’ cabins, athletic fields, basketball and volleyball courts, zip lines, a Ranch House (dining hall), the Sugar Shack (camp store), a rodeo arena, and, of course, the clean, cool Frio River to play in.

A Day In The Life Of A Camper

No two days are exactly the same. We strive to be unpredictable and spontaneous, but the schedule below gives you our starting point.

9:00 a.m. – Breakfast

That’s right. We don’t get up early at Echo Valley. Find out more about Camp Food.

9:45 a.m. – MOG and Rooted

Boys and girls split for morning devotional and quiet time. The Guys’ time is called MOG (Men of God) and the Girls’ time is called Rooted.

11:00 a.m. – First Activity

Campers go to the activity that they signed up for at the beginning of the week. Check out our activities here!

11:50 a.m. – Sugar Shack

Campers have free time to enjoy snacks at our camp store, the Sugar Shack.

12:10 p.m. – Second Activity

Campers go to the activity that they signed up for at the beginning of the week. Check out our activities here!

1:00 p.m. – Lunch

Find out more about Camp Food.

2:00 p.m. – Rest Time

This is a time for campers to sleep, read, or write letters in their cabins.

3:00 p.m. – Group Games/Cabin Activity

For Group Games, campers will be placed on one of our four teams — Tejas, Outlaws, Bounty Hunters, and Texas Rangers. They face off against each other in fun and competitive team games (e.g., Dodge Ball, Capture the Flag, Jailbreak, Ball-in-net). At the end of the session, two awards are given out — one for the most team spirit (the Sullivan award), and one for the most wins (the Cody award).

For Cabin Activities, cabin counselors plan a fun activity for the entire cabin (e.g., going to a special spot in the Canyon in a truck, swimming together, playing a game, etc.).

4:30 p.m. – Frio Fiesta

Time for all of camp to play at the Echo Valley waterfront, either swimming, floating in tubes, playing on our water toy, water slide, and zipline, or just hanging out.

5:30 p.m. – Free Time

Campers enjoy some free time to play games such as washers, 9-square, corn hole, soccer, basketball, etc. They often just hang out with each other around the Ranch House and catch up after the day’s events.

6:00 p.m. – Dinner

Find out more about Camp Food.

6:45 - After Dinner Free Time

Campers relax during “golden hour” as the sun is setting over the Canyon walls. They play games such as washers, 9-square, corn hole, soccer, basketball, etc. They often just hang out with each other around the Ranch House and catch up after the day’s events.

7:00 p.m. — Sugar Shack

Free time continues and our camp store, the Sugar Shack opens so that campers can enjoy snacks, drinks or purchase supplies or LLYC merch!

7:30 p.m. – Roundup

Camp gathers together in the Pavilion for songs, skits, games, chaos, and a chance to listen to a spiritual message presented by a one of our leadership staff.

8:45 p.m. – Cabin Time

Campers gather with their cabins in a smaller, more intimate setting to talk with their counselors and reflect upon what they learned at Roundup.

9:30 p.m. – Late-Night Activity

After cabin time, we usually get into some late-night shenanigans. It could be a dance, night-time group game, cabin activity, carnival…you know the good stuff!
