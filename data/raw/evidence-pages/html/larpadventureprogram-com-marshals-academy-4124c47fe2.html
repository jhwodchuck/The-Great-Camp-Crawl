    Join the Marshals Academy®

    Lead. Create. Inspire.

    Become a hero in your community!

    The Marshals Academy® is the LARP Adventure Program’s advanced leadership course for high schoolers.

    Through immersive storytelling, teens build real-world skills in leadership, emotional intelligence, and event production—whether mentoring, creating LARP experiences, or managing behind-the-scenes logistics.

    Core Values

    Modesty · Courtesy
    Integrity · Self-Control
    Perseverance · Indomitable Spirit

    There are two Leadership Tracks to choose from

    Track 1: CITing & Mentoring
    Track 2: Event Production & NPCing
    Hey LARP Parents! PSST. Click Here!

    Track 1:

    CITing & Classroom Mentoring

    For students ready to mentor younger participants and take on meaningful leadership roles in LAP’s after-school and summer programs. Recommended for students in grades 9-11 who want to remain PCs during our weekend event season.

    Focus Areas:

    • Mentoring in classrooms and day camps
    • Supporting staff and co-teaching curriculum and games
    • Modeling safe, respectful, and inclusive behavior
    • Practicing emotional intelligence in real-time situations
    • Applying The Four Agreements and community values

    New Curriculum for 2025:

    • Mentorship Program: Paired with a staff mentor for guidance, feedback, and support
    • Leadership Workshops: Formal training in communication, facilitation, and resume-building
    • CPR/First Aid Certification: Required to participate; LAP provides access and discounts
    • Defined Teaching Roles: CITs serve as assistant instructors and peer mentors
    • Portfolio & Reference Support: Each CIT builds a leadership portfolio and may receive staff recommendations

    Progress & Recognition:

    • Tiered Pathways: CIT 1 → CIT 2 → Junior Staff
    • Completion Certificate + digital badge for portfolios or resumes
    • End-of-Year Reflection & Growth Review

    Bonus Opportunity:

    • FREE CIT Placement at LAP After-School or Summer Camps the following year after successful completion

    Requirements

    • Participation in weekly HS LARP Class (Wednesdays, 6–8 PM)
    • 36 classroom service hours OR 2 weeks of day camp
    • Active CPR/First Aid/AED certification (This training will be done as part of the class. An additional fee may be required to get a certificate.)

    How to register for Track 1:

    For Winter & Spring: Applications are open! We invite all enthusiastic and motivated high school students to apply!

    • Our selection process ensures we bring together a diverse group of students ready to embark on this adventure.
    • Once we review your application we will reach out to you and your parents to schedule a virtual interview with you!
    • After the interview we will send you the instructions for the registering and paying the tuition for this program.
    • Tuition for the school year (10 months):
      • $195 a month or $1852.50* for the school year (September 2025 to June 2026)
        * This will be pro-rated based on start date. Sliding scale/payment plans available!

    Contact jenn@larpadventureprogram.com with any questions.

    Track 2:

    Event Production & NPC Leadership

    For students interested in immersive design, logistics, production leadership, and running events like Etheraz II from backstage to battlefield. Recommended for students in grades 9-12.

    Focus Areas:

    • Leading NPCs and small teams in roleplay scenarios
    • Designing props, lighting, costuming, and sets (scenography)
    • Learning field mechanics and narrative embodiment
    • Managing logistics stations and event gear (Monster Camp, radios, wipe boards)
    • Building inclusive, safe, and emotionally rich player experiences

    New Curriculum for 2025:

    • Mentor Matching: Paired with event leads in props, logistics, directing, etc.
    • Project-Based Learning: Real assignments (e.g., stat monsters, light a scene, run a mod)
    • Leadership Lab Sessions: Optional training in ICS, accessibility, storytelling, and field management
    • Professional Development: Resume language and references from experienced staff
    • Accessibility Training: Tools and strategies to build inclusive game design

    Progress & Recognition:

    • Event Production Badges in props, field mech, costuming, etc.
    • Portfolio Credit for completed projects and scene design
    • CIT 2 / Junior Producer status available for high-performing students

    Bonus Opportunity:

    • DISCOUNTED NPC Registration at LAP Weekend & Overnight Events upon successful track completion
    • FA/CPR AED training will be done as part of the class (Additional fee may be required to get a certificate.)

    Requirements

    • Participation in weekly HS LARP Class (Wednesdays, 6–8 PM)
    • Attend at least one overnight event
    • Join current weekly pre-event planning meetings.
    • Optional support for day events or seasonal modules

    How to register for Track 2:

    For Winter & Spring: Applications are open! We invite all enthusiastic and motivated high school students to apply!

    • Our selection process ensures we bring together a diverse group of students ready to embark on this adventure.
    • Once we review your application, we will reach out to you and your parents to schedule a virtual interview with you!
    • After the interview, we will send you the instructions for registering and paying the tuition for this program.
    • Tuition for the school year (10 months):
      • $195 a month or $1852.50* for the school year (September 2025 to June 2026)
        * This will be pro-rated based on start date. Sliding scale/payment plans available!

    Contact jenn@larpadventureprogram.com with any questions.
