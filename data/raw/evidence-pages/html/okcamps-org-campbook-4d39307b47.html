    FAQS

    Registration

    Q: How do I register?

    A: Register now by clicking here.

    Q: How do I know what camps my child is eligible for?

    A: All of our camps are separated by grade. Whatever grade your child will be entering in the Fall of 2026 is the grade you will use to determine what camps they can go to.

    Q: What if I need to cancel my registration?

    A: Please communicate all cancellations to OK Camps via phone at (405) 530-2035. Any registrations that are not cancelled through the camps office prior to 10 business days before the camp starts will still be held responsible for payment. At this point, meals and materials will have already been ordered for all registered participants.

    Finances

    Q: Can I pay for all my campers with one check?

    A: Yes! Please send a note with camper names and camps attending with your check. Please note that to receive early bird pricing, each camper MUST be paid by the posted April 30th deadline.

    Q: What if I need a refund?

    A: We are able to issue refunds; however, there is a $25 processing fee that will be withheld. If less than 10 business days’ notice is given, no refunds will be issued, except in limited circumstances. If camp is cancelled for any reason, a full refund will be issued.

    Q: What do I do if my church is paying for my camper?

    A: You can register your camper like normal! Just enter your church’s transfer code when you get to the payment screen.

    Q: What is a transfer code?

    A: In our new system, each church has a code that allows us to link your account to your church. This lets them see which kids are coming and pay online, if they would like.

    Q: Why is it asking me for a deposit?

    A: To ensure that everyone is fully registered, they must either pay the $5 deposit to hold their spot or enter their church transfer code.

    Q: Are there scholarships?

    A: Please contact your local United Methodist Church first to check for local scholarship opportunities. A limited number of scholarships are available upon request. Please check out okcamps.org/scholarships for all our opportunities.

    Camp

    Q: What does my child need to bring to camp?

    A: Here is our general packing list! Confirmation emails with specific packing instructions should be emailed closer to the start of camp!

    Q: Do I need to send leaders with my group?

    A: Most Likely Yes! Contact the camp dean to find out if they are in need of leaders, and what the minimum leaders required for your campers will be. If you do not have any leaders to send with your campers, please contact the dean and arrangements can be made. If you are sending adults to camp, please ensure they register AT LEAST 3 weeks prior to camp. All leaders and volunteers over the age of 18 will need a valid background check and training in order to attend camp, which must be administered 2-3 weeks prior to the camp start date.

    Q: Who are the adult leaders at each camp?

    A: All adult deans and leaders are volunteers who have completed a background check and references on file with the OK Camps office, as well as basic Safe Sanctuary/Sexual Abuse Awareness training.

    General

    Q: Do you have insurance for my campers?

    A: Yes! Every camper is covered by insurance for emergency medical up to $3,500 per accident and $1,000 for sickness. This camper insurance policy is limited and is secondary to campers personal insurance. It will not cover pre-existing conditions, nervous or mental disorders, broken glasses, dental work, hearing aids, or other such items.

    Q: How can I get involved with camping ministries?

    A:

    Volunteering – We love volunteers! We are always looking for camp nurses, leaders, and leaders in training. Contact camp deans or the conference office if you are interested in helping at a camp.
    Donating – Your donations provide valuable scholarships and resources for our camps. This allows them to continue transforming lives. You may mail a check to the conference office or visit the “ways to Give” section for more information.
    Praying – All of our campers, leaders, and staff greatly appreciate your prayers not only during the summer but throughout the year.

    Q: How can I promote camps in my church?

    A: We love when you promote camps in your church andthere are several ways to do so! Contact the camps office if you are interested in having a “Camp Sunday” or would like to have someone come speak to your congregation or parents.
