Aggie Enrichment Summer Camps

North Carolina A&T State University offers a variety of summer programs to inspire the next generations of Aggies. The camps allow young people to explore, learn new skills, prepare for the future – and have fun! Check back frequently as we finalize the camps.

N.C. A&T departments: Registering your camp

All departments (academic, grant-funded, athletic, and enrichment) offering summer camp programs must register their programs. Register your summer camp program. All program directors, staff, and volunteers who work with minors must adhere to the Protection of Minors policy and attend required online Protection of Minors training.

Program Information

About: (Grades 3-5) Discover, Explore, and Build Confidence in Math! This one-week camp helps students strengthen foundational math skills through engaging, hands-on activities aligned with North Carolina’s Mathematics Standards. Campers will explore number sense, operations, fractions, measurement, and geometry using puzzles, games, and real-world applications. Each activity is designed to build conceptual understanding, promote mathematical reasoning, and encourage collaboration.
Focus Areas (Aligned with NC Math Standards):
• Operations & Algebraic Thinking: Exploring multiplication, division, and patterns in numbers.
• Number & Operations—Fractions: Understanding parts of a whole through visuals and models.
• Measurement & Data: Applying math to time, volume, and data collection challenges.
• Geometry: Investigating shapes, angles, and spatial reasoning in fun, creative ways.

About: (Grades 6-8) Think Critically. Apply Strategically. Explore Deeply. This one-week camp is designed for middle school students ready to deepen their mathematical understanding through interactive lessons and projects aligned with North Carolina’s Mathematics Standards. Students will tackle real-world challenges that build algebraic reasoning, proportional thinking, and geometric problem-solving skills. Through group investigations and strategy-based games, campers will strengthen their ability to analyze, explain, and apply mathematical concepts with confidence.
Focus Areas (Aligned with NC Math Standards):
• Ratios & Proportional Relationships: Applying ratios and rates to solve everyday problems. • Expressions & Equations: Building fluency in algebraic reasoning and equation solving.
• Geometry: Exploring angles, area, surface area, and volume through hands-on design activities.
• Statistics & Probability: Interpreting data and understanding variability in real contexts.

About: This summer, The College of Science and Technology (CoST) will offer an interdisciplinary summer camp to expose underserved students to science, technology, and mathematics. The camp provides 10 days of hands-on learning experience focused on project-based, interdisciplinary STEM education. Participants will engage in science, mathematics, and technology through instruction and immersive activities to build knowledge and confidence. Running six hours daily, the camp includes lunch, community-building activities, college preparation sessions, and exciting science and tech experiences—like coding, programming, networking, drones, robotics, game design, and scientific discovery and experimental methods. Each day introduces a new area of STEM, allowing students to explore diverse scientific fields with focused instruction: for example, Biology on Monday, Chemistry on Tuesday, Physics on Wednesday, Geomatics on Thursday, and so on. Mathematics is incorporated daily, reinforcing its foundational role in all areas of STEM. Participants will learn about STEM career paths and gain insights into strategies for college success. Designed to be a welcoming environment for high school students, the camp fosters learning among peers with similar backgrounds. Mentorship from students and faculty in the College of Science and Technology provides representation and inspiration, encouraging participants to envision themselves in STEM careers. The camp serves 30 rising sophomores, juniors, and seniors, aiming to cultivate or deepen a love for STEM while inspiring participants to consider educational and professional futures in these fields and potentially matriculate in one of CoST's majors.

This summer, the Center of Excellence in Entrepreneurship and Innovation (CEEI) at North Carolina A&T State University invites high school students to take part in an exciting, immersive experience through our annual Entrepreneurs & Innovators NexGen Residential Summer Camp—continuing our commitment to hands-on learning, innovation, and entrepreneurial exploration.
 
Program Overview
For Summer 2026, the Entrepreneurs & Innovators NexGen Residential Summer Camp will be led by the Center of Excellence in Entrepreneurship & Innovation at North Carolina A&T State University and hosted at University of North Carolina at Greensboro. 
 
This fun, interactive, and engaging residential camp is designed for high school students who are curious about entrepreneurship, innovation, and future career pathways.
Participants will engage in a dynamic, hands-on learning experience designed to spark curiosity, creativity, and confidence. Throughout the program, students will:
  • Develop an entrepreneurial mindset grounded in problem-solving, innovation, and opportunity recognition
  • Learn how to identify real-world challenges and explore solutions that meet genuine market needs
  • Collaborate with peers to develop, refine, and confidently present a business idea
Beyond the classroom, students will take part in a variety of interactive experiences that blend learning with fun. The camp experience incorporates experiential learning opportunities, exposure to entrepreneurial environments, and engaging social activities that encourage teamwork, creativity, and connection.
Together, these elements create an energetic, supportive environment where students learn, build, and have fun—while gaining a meaningful introduction to entrepreneurship and innovation as future career pathways.

Camp Dates
Session 1: Sunday, June 14, 2026 – Friday, June 19, 2026: APPLY NOW
Session 2: Sunday, June 21, 2026 – Friday, June 26, 2026: APPLY NOW
 
Eligibility & Application Process
This year, students must apply and be selected to participate.
Requirements:
  • Minimum 2.8 GPA
  • Applications will be reviewed through a competitive selection process
Capacity per session:
  • Minimum: 28 campers
  • Maximum: 40 campers
Application Deadlines:
  • Session 1: Applications due by April 3, 2026
  • Session 2: Applications due by April 10, 2026
 
Acceptance, Payment & Waitlist
Acceptance letters will be sent via email.

Once accepted, families will have three (3) business days to submit full payment to secure the student’s spot.
 
If payment is not received within three (3) business days, the student will be removed from the acceptance list and the spot will be offered to students on the waitlist.
 
Cost
$1,200 per camper
 
Refund Policy
If a student is accepted and an unexpected or unforeseen circumstance prevents attendance, the Center of Excellence in Entrepreneurship & Innovation will offer a 50% refund, provided the request is submitted on or before the applicable refund deadline listed below.
Refund requests must be submitted in writing and are subject to review.
Refund deadlines:
  • Session 1: Refund requests must be received by May 4, 2026
  • Session 2: Refund requests must be received by May 11, 2026
No refunds will be issued for any reason after the applicable refund deadline.

This includes, but is not limited to, scheduling conflicts, travel plans, illness, dismissal from the program, or early departure.

By submitting payment, families acknowledge and agree to the refund policy as stated above.
