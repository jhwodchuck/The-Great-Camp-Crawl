FAQs

Have questions about joining the Y? See answers to commonly asked questions below.

Is there an annual contract when I sign up for a membership?

No, we do not have contracts at the Y. You can cancel your membership at any time.

How much will it cost to join today?

Since monthly membership drafts happen on the 1st or 15th (you get to decide your draft date when you join), your first month’s membership fee will be prorated. The price you pay when joining includes a joining fee (if applicable) and the prorated membership amount. For example, if you were to sign up for a household membership on the 20th, you would pay the one-time joining fee price of $105, plus the prorated monthly fee (either roughly $30.19 for a draft date on the 1st or $65.42 for a draft date on the 15th).

Can I take a tour before I join?

Yes! We encourage you to stop by the Y to check out the facility and learn more about what a membership at the Y could mean for you. Find a Y near you. 

Who can I add to my membership?

You can add an additional adult to your membership for $25 a month. If you live with the individual, it may be more cost-effective to upgrade your membership to a household membership.

Does my insurance plan offer a YMCA membership?

Your insurance plan likely works with a third party to provide you with a gym membership plan add-on. Through this add-on, you could be eligible for a YMCA membership. These add-ons include Ambetter of Oklahoma, Oklahoma Complete Health, Renew Active, Silver & Fit, Active & Fit, SilverSneakers, Well onTarget, Blue365 and Prime. Stop by your local Y to activate your insurance-based membership.

Does the YMCA offer memberships through Medicaid? (SoonerCare, SoonerSelect, etc.)

Yes, SoonerSelect, Oklahoma’s Medicaid managed care program, offers gym membership benefits as part of its value-added services. These benefits are designed to promote physical activity and healthy lifestyles among members.

Check with your plan provider to determine what value-added benefits are available to use at the YMCA.

  • Aetna Better Health® of Oklahoma
  • Humana Healthy Horizons®
  • Oklahoma Complete Health
Does the YMCA accept Sooner SUCCESS vouchers?

Yes, the YMCA of Greater Oklahoma City accepts Sooner SUCCESS vouchers as a form of payment for memberships and programs. This collaboration supports caregivers of children and adults with intellectual and developmental disabilities (I/DD), and/or special health care needs by providing access to wellness and respite services.

 Sooner SUCCESS works intimately with both the public and private sectors with the overarching goal to promote a comprehensive, coordinated system of health, social and educational services for Oklahoma children and youth with special healthcare needs in their local community.

 How to Use Sooner SUCCESS Vouchers at the YMCA

  1. Verify Eligibility: Ensure you qualify for Sooner SUCCESS Lifespan Respite Vouchers or Adaptive Equipment and Professional Services Program. For Lifespan Respite Vouchers, eligibility is typically based on being a caregiver to an individual with I/DD. For the Adaptive Equipment and Professional Services Program, eligibility is for children birth to age 17, with a special health care need. For more information, contact Sooner SUCCESS at (405) 271-2710 or email sooner-success@ouhsc.edu. oklahoma.gov
  2. Contact the YMCA: Reach out to your local YMCA of Greater Oklahoma City branch to inquire about using Sooner SUCCESS vouchers for membership or programs. Each branch may have specific procedures for processing these vouchers.
  3. Provide Necessary Documentation: When contacting the YMCA, be prepared to provide documentation of your eligibility for Sooner SUCCESS vouchers and any required forms or identification.
  4. Activate Your Membership: Once your voucher is processed, you can activate your YMCA membership and begin utilizing the facilities and programs available.

This partnership between Sooner SUCCESS and the YMCA aims to enhance the well-being of caregivers and individuals with I/DD by providing access to health, wellness, and respite services. https://soonersuccess.ouhsc.edu/

Do you accept Self-Directed Services (SDS) vouchers?

Yes, the YMCA of Greater Oklahoma City accepts Self-Directed Services (SDS) vouchers. You will need to set up a time to activate your YMCA membership with your local Y’s membership director. Contact the Y directly here.

Can I use the Y with wellness platforms offered by my employer? (Wellhub, HUSK, etc.)

Yes. Check with your employer to see if they offer wellness benefits through programs like Wellhub (formerly Gympass), HUSK (formerly GlobalFit), or similar platforms. These programs often include YMCA memberships at little or no cost to you. Your HR or benefits department can tell you if you’re eligible and how to get started.

How do I know if I qualify for a corporate membership?

View our current corporate partners here or check with your employer. Your HR or benefits department can tell you if you’re eligible and how to get started. 

Interested in joining as a corporate partner? Fill out the interest form to get started.

Do you offer discounts to foster families?

Yes, the YMCA of Greater Oklahoma City offers a Foster Care Benefit Program in partnership with the Oklahoma Alliance of YMCAs and the Oklahoma Department of Human Services (DHS). This initiative provides certified foster families across Oklahoma with discounted access to YMCA services, supporting their efforts in nurturing and caring for children in state custody.

Must present foster care benefit card to the front desk in order to receive the discount.

How do I know if I qualify for financial assistance?

Financial assistance is based on annual income. Must present proper documentation to apply. 

What ages do you provide child care for while I am working out?

Most YMCAs offer free child care for up to two hours while you work out for ages 6 weeks to 7 years. Some locations have supervised areas for 8-12 year-olds, as well.

Can I bring guests with me with my membership?

If you have a friend looking to join the Y, they can request a free trial pass by filling out this form. After your first year with the Y, you’ll receive one day pass every year you can use to share the Y with a friend. Day passes are available for purchase. $5 for youth, $10 for an adult, and $25 for a family.

How do I cancel my membership?

Please contact your local YMCA to cancel. 

If I cancel, will I still receive program discounts?

No, program discounts are reserved for YMCA members. If you are currently enrolled in a recurring program and receive a discount for having a membership, you will be charged the non-member price once your membership is terminated.

How do I sign up for 24/7 access?

Please visit our 24/7 access page to opt in for 24/7 access.

How do I check in to the Y?

The easiest way to check in is by using the barcode found in the YMCA360 app. You can also purchase a plastic key tag that will have a barcode associated with your membership on it.

Wellhub members must use the Wellhub app to check in.

Which locations can I visit?

If you have an association-wide membership (this type of membership excludes branch-only memberships and the digital membership), you can access 14 of our 16 YMCA locations. If you are at least 50 years old, you can also access YMCA Healthy Living Center (50+) and Pete White YMCA (50+).

Do group fitness classes cost extra?

No, our group fitness classes are included in your membership. The classes listed on the schedule are free to attend, and you can view those here.

We also offer separate paid specialty classes and clinics you can register for, if interested.

Can I visit a Y in another state?

Yes! If you have a household, adult or young adult membership with us, you can visit any Y in the country, as long as they participate in the nationwide membership program. We encourage you to call ahead to ensure you’ll be able to access their facility.
