About Our Program

The MFA Directing Program at Texas State cultivates directors to become creative leaders in the American theatre. Through focused training in text analysis, point of view, style, and hands-on production experience, TXST directors deepen their craft and clarify their directorial voice.

We accept two MFA directors each year. The program is fully funded and includes a tuition waiver and paid assistantship.

Admission Requirements

There are two ways to apply to TXST’s MFA Directing Program:

Option 1: Participate in URTA.

You must have completed your application to the university before we can schedule a callback. Apply declaring your major as “Pre-Theatre" or “Theatre.”

We request you also apply to the Honors College upon acceptance into the university if you meet the criteria.

Option 2: Apply to TXST Directly

  1. Apply to the Graduate College
    1. You must complete your application to the Graduate College before we can schedule an initial video interview or campus visit.
    2. Please note, we cannot waive the non-refundable University application fee.
  2. Application Requirements
    1. Resume/CV
    2. Statement of purpose detailing your area of specialty, related background information, and goals in pursuing an MFA in Theatre, Directing
    3. Theatre portfolio (website or digital) which may include rehearsal prompt books of past directing projects, production photos, video links and reviews. 
    4. Two letters of recommendation
    5. Any other requirements specified by the Graduate Program.

Assistantships

Graduate Instructional Assistantships (GIA) and Graduate Teaching Assistantships (GTA) assignments are made each term and range from Instructor of Record, Teaching Assistant, and production support staff, based on each student’s skills and interests.

Each student is awarded two 25% assistantship positions (10 hours each) for a total of 20-hours per week.

To remain eligible for scholarships and assistantships, students must hold a cumulative GPA of 3.0 or higher and remain in good standing

For additional information, visit the Graduate College Website.

Opportunities

Expand FAQ List

  • Year One

    • Assistant Direct a Mainstage Production

      Work alongside a faculty or guest director to learn the design and rehearsal process at TXST.

    • Direct a Reading for the New Works Festival

      One-week collaboration and workshop process for a new play, culminating in a staged reading in the Performing Arts Center’s Recital Hall or other venue.

    • First Year Directing Project

      Direct a One-Act play in a black box classroom space. This self-produced project is focused on clear storytelling and working with actors.

  • Year Two

    • Submit to the KCACTF SDC Directing Initiative

      Represent TXST in this national SDC-sponsored competition and receive feedback from leading professionals.

    • Direct a Fully Produced Production in the Studio Theatre

      Stage your first fully produced show in our beloved and intimate thrust space as part of the Theatre Season.

  • Year Three

    • Shakespeare Showcase

      Direct a substantial selection from a Shakespeare play, featuring senior BFA actors in a special capstone presentation.

    • Direct a Fully Produced Production in the Mainstage or Performing Arts Center

      Cap your training by directing a fully produced show in one of TXST’s large-scale performance venues.
