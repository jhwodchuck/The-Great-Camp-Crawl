# Contact Us

![Aerial shot of Moby Arena](https://admissions.colostate.edu/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/moby-aerial-hero.jpg)

When we say we’re here for you, we mean it. The Office of Admissions is dedicated to not only answering your questions but anticipating the ones you haven’t thought of yet.

Whether you have a question about submitting your application or what it’s like to be a student at Colorado State University, we have dedicated teams ready to help. We provide a variety of ways to reach out, so you get the info you need, the way you need it.

## What info do you need?

We can help you the most when we know who you are and the info you’re looking for. Please choose the link below that fits you best to reach the specialists for that area.

Includes freshman, transfer, second-bachelor, returner, and non-degree/visiting students

[INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS](#international)

Includes international undergraduate OR graduate students (Master’s and Ph.D.) and English proficiency, international credentials/degree equivalents, visa/immigration documents, international community.

[GRADUATE STUDENTS](#grads)

Includes domestic AND international master’s and Ph.D. students

## Contact info for graduate student questions

Getting help with graduate school admissions

The graduate admission process at CSU is a partnership between the Graduate School, graduate academic departments, the International Enrollment Center, and the Office of Admissions. Follow the general guidelines outlined below to contact experts with answers to your questions.

[Check out our frequently asked questions](#faq) to see whether we have a ready answer for your question, or contact us using the info below.

### Graduate school

Get a [quick introduction to CSU and referrals to other resources](https://graduateschool.colostate.edu/future-students/) that can support you during the admission process and beyond.

Review [general information about application requirements](https://graduateschool.colostate.edu/apply/), including document submission instructions and options to apply for free.

### Academic department

[Graduate academic departments](https://graduateschool.colostate.edu/programs/) establish their own deadlines, document requirements, and selection criteria. Rely on the academic department for information about the academic program, application deadlines, admission criteria, decision timelines, funding support, program and term changes, and next steps after admission. Your department will be your primary point of contact.

### International Enrollment Center

Contact the [International Enrollment Center](https://international.colostate.edu/iec/contact/) for questions about international credentials and degree equivalents, English proficiency requirements, immigration/ student visas, and the international community at CSU. Email [[email protected]](/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#3f56514b5a4d515e4b5650515e537f5c5053504c4b5e4b5a115a5b4a) or call (970) 492-4686. ## FAQs

I don’t know what type of applicant I am. How do I find out?

All [applicant types are explained online](https://admissions.colostate.edu/apply/). You can find out whether you are a freshman, transfer, returning, second bachelor’s, international, graduate, or nondegree-seeking applicant.

When do applications open and what are your deadlines?

Application launch dates and deadlines are based on your applicant type and the semester you want to start classes. [Find your applicant type](https://admissions.colostate.edu/apply/) and then click into the application guide to find out when you should apply.

How do I log into CSU systems like RAMweb?

To log into CSU’s electronic systems, you will need to [activate your NetID](https://admissions.colostate.edu/apply/student-id-university-email/).

Undergraduate applicants receive instructions to activate NetID by email within 1-2 days after submitting an application.

Graduate students receive instructions to activate NetID as part of their notice of admission.

How do I know my application status?

Undergraduate student instructions: [check your application status online](https://admissions.colostate.edu/apply/application-status/) using the undergraduate admissions dashboard.

Graduate student instructions: Log into your [graduate admissions dashboard](http://gradadmissions.colostate.edu/status) using the email and password you used to apply. Your dashboard may include program deadlines and review timelines.

How do I send documents to complete my application?

A limited range of materials can be uploaded to your admissions dashboard. Materials that you upload will be reflected within 15-30 minutes.

Some materials, such as transcripts, may be accepted only by mail or through a secure server. Allow 3-5 business days *after they have been received by CSU* for materials submitted via postal mail, express delivery, or a certified, secure electronic document service to be reflected in your dashboard. If it has been more than five business days and the material does not show as received, contact us with the date and method of submission, name(s) on the document and third-party confirmation number/receipt (if applicable).

- [Undergraduate student instructions](https://admissions.colostate.edu/apply/submitting-documents/)
- [Graduate student instructions](https://graduateschool.colostate.edu/apply/)

Undergraduate students only: Will my credits transfer to CSU?

Learn more about [how credits transfer to CSU](https://admissions.colostate.edu/apply/special-circumstances/transfer-credits/), whether you’ve taken college classes in high school, enrolled at another college or university, or have earned your associate’s degree.

Graduate students only: What are the GRE/GMAT requirements?

Contact the academic department directly for details on their program’s score requirements or to request a waiver.

Graduate students only: How can I submit revised materials or change my recommender?

Use the Upload Materials section of your [graduate admissions dashboard](http://gradadmissions.colostate.edu/status) to submit new or updated documents. Use the Manage Recommenders link if you need to change or send reminders to your recommenders. Recommendations cannot be uploaded by applicants. Note: once a graduate application has moved forward for review by the department, additional documents are not accepted.
