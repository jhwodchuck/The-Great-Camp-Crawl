# LEADERSHIP PROGRAMS

![Three girls with face paint make faces into the camera.](/sites/default/files/styles/ping_pong/public/2025-02/IMG_7364.jpg.webp?itok=YGzJcLFP)

There are four leadership program options: Junior Leader-In-Training, Leader-In-Training, Counselor-In-Training and Intern. All leadership program campers will stay in our new platform tents with up to nine other teens. Participants are not permitted to stay over the weekend in between sessions. Age ranges for these programs are approximate: Each participant will be evaluated on a case-by-case basis to ensure placement in the best-fitting program. You can find dates and rates below.

[Apply](https://www.ymcanoco.org/camp-tumbleson-lake/ldp-application)

####

Rate: $993
*Y members receive a $50 credit usable at the Camp Store or Snack Shack.*

**Session 2, Session 3, Session 5, Session 6, Session 7, Session 8, Session 9**

Our Junior Leader in Training program is the first step of a teen's leadership journey at Camp Tumbleson Lake and is for teens who have completed the 8th grade. Junior LITs have the opportunity to shadow counselors and spend time with our youngest campers to understand how our staff foster the magic of camp. They will also have time to enjoy camp classics such as high ropes, playing on our inflatable obstacle course, paddling on the lake, and also savoring the traditions of Breakfast of Champions, bead ceremony, evening activities, and more. While working directly with our frontline summer staff, Junior LITs have the opportunity to become role models, lead with experience, foster new friendships and nurture confidence and independence.

####

Rate: $893
*Y members receive a $50 credit usable at the Camp Store or Snack Shack.*

**Session 2, Session 3, Session 5, Session 6, Session 7, Session 8, Session 9**

Our Leader in Training program is for teens who have completed the 9th grade and want to evolve as a leader, build confidence and make an impact—while having a fun, sunshine-soaked experience at camp. While surrounded by a welcoming community of campers, other leaders and staff, LITs learn about the needs of children in a camp setting and gain more practical knowledge about camp's functions. This program is highlighted by the opportunity for LITs to shadow and assist staff members in multiple activity areas, while also having time to join in on camp activities with their friends. LITs will also participate in Camp Tumbleson Lake traditions such as Breakfast of Champions, bead ceremony, Battle for the Paddle, and opening and closing campfires. LITs find guidance and support from Camp Counselors and Leadership Staff.

####

Rate: $793
*Y members receive a $50 credit usable at the Camp Store or Snack Shack.*

**Session 2, Session 3, Session 5, Session 6, Session 7, Session 8, Session 9**

Our Counselor in Training program is for teens who have completed the 10th grade and who want to push themselves and take a step into the life of a counselor. Previous Leadership Development Program experience is not required. Through opportunities to focus on one to three specialty areas to gain knowledge and experience. CITs grow to understand age characteristics, facilitation and progressive activity planning. This experience provides the opportunity to assist in planning and facilitating all-camp events and closing campfire under the guidance of the Camp Tumbleson Lake program staff members. CITs will participate in the tradition of Breakfast of Champions, opening campfire, Battle for the Paddle and bead ceremony. They will be invited to join in on some camp activities to savor the pure fun of the camper experience. They will find support from a variety of staff members with various leadership styles on the YMCA Camp Tumbleson Lake team, from the Counselors and Program Instructors to Leadership Staff.

####

No cost.

**Session 2, Session 3, Session 5, Session 6, Session 7, Session 8, Session 9**

Our Intern program is the final step in the Leadership Development Program at Camp Tumbleson Lake and is for teens who have completed the 11th grade. Previous experience in the Leadership Development Program is preferred but not required. During this program, teens work with campers of all ages in our traditional programs. Our interns have a hand in designing their experience as it is their last year as a camper, allowing them to continue to grow, learn and nurture their confidence and skill as a leader. This final step in their leadership journey culminates in a well-rounded knowledge of the day in the life of a counselor as they take on more independence and help in leading progressive program facilitation in various specialty areas. Interns also are invited to help lead evening programs and closing campfire alongside seasoned program staff members. If your teen is looking for a summer like no other and the opportunity to give back to their Camp Tumbleson Lake community, this is the program for them.
