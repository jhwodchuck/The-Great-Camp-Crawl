# Dates & Tuition

We’re so glad you’ve found your way here! Enrollment at Cheley is first-come, first-served, and availability shifts from year to year. Certain Summer Camp units may fill in the fall, Quarter B-4 can be unpredictable, and Family Camp often fills right when enrollment opens. Waitlists generally begin forming in the months that follow, so we encourage families to register early to secure a spot. Once a program reaches capacity, we establish a waitlist in the order applications are received.

If you’re unsure which program or unit is the best fit, our Enrollment Manager is always happy to help. Below, you’ll find the dates, tuition, and details you need to start making your camper’s summer at Cheley a reality.

## Summer Camp 2026

### Our 27-Day Program for 9- to 17-year-olds

The Cheley Experience is designed with a clear beginning, middle, and end—giving campers the time and space to truly settle in, grow, and connect. Our flagship program is a 27-day traditional overnight summer camp, offered in two sessions: **First Term** and **Second Term**. Each session includes **eight camp units**, creating close-knit communities within the larger Cheley community. This four-week structure allows campers to unplug, immerse themselves in the Colorado Rockies, form deep friendships, and build new skills. From day one, campers begin forming connections, and we close each term with meaningful reflection and closure activities. Most campers attend for a single term, while some returning campers choose to stay for both. Because camp is built around shared experiences from start to finish, late arrivals and early departures are strongly discouraged, as they can impact both your camper’s experience and the group dynamic. If the dates don’t align with your summer plans, we encourage you to consider switching terms or postponing enrollment.

**To attend Summer Camp, campers must be between 9 and 17 years old by August 15, 2026, with occasional exceptions.** Unit placement is based on the age of a camper during their session. If a camper’s birthday falls during camp or near August 15, we’ll work with families to determine the best unit placement, considering age, grade, and readiness. Not sure which unit is the best fit for your camper? We’re happy to help—just reach out! Some units do fill quickly, so we recommend enrolling early to secure your preferred spot.

## Quarter B-4 2026

### Our 5-Day Program for 8- to 10-year-olds

For campers who are excited about Cheley but either too young or not quite ready for a full four weeks away, we offer Quarter B-4: a fun, **four-night** overnight camp experience designed as a gentle introduction to the Cheley Experience. It’s a great way for 8- to 10-year-olds to dip their toes into camp life before committing to our full 27-day program. That said, Quarter B-4 is intended as a stepping stone, not a long-term alternative

**To attend Quarter B-4, campers must be between 8 and 10 years old by the last day of the program.** Once a camper turns 11 years old, they must move into our full 27-Day Summer Camp program, as we do not offer shorter sessions for older campers. Families of 9- and 10-year-olds can choose between Quarter B-4 and Summer Camp based on their camper’s readiness. If you’re unsure which session is the best fit (or just want to talk it through), we’re always here to help—just give us a call!

### Discounts

If you register before November 1st for Summer Camp, you will receive a $200 early-bird discount. If you pay in full by January 15th for Summer Camp, you will receive a $75 discount. Siblings, not including the first child, will receive a $150 discount for Summer Camp and a $50 discount for Quarter B-4.

### Payment Options & Schedule

We understand that every family’s financial situation is unique; therefore, we offer flexible payment plans to accommodate your preferences. All payments must be completed by May 1st to secure your camper’s enrollment. Failure to meet this deadline may result in your camper’s withdrawal from camp. You have the following payment choices when enrolling:

- **Full Balance Payment:** Families can choose to pay the entire tuition upfront.
- **Deposit and Three Installments:** Opt for a deposit at enrollment and split the remaining balance into three installments due on January 1st, March 1st, and May 1st.
- **Deposit and Monthly Installments:** Spread the cost by paying a deposit upon enrollment, followed by monthly installments. Payments will be due each month from the time of enrollment until May 1st.
- **Deposit and Confirm Payment Schedule Later:** Pay a deposit upon enrollment to secure your camper’s spot, then confirm your payment schedule at a later date.

We accept various payment methods, including ACH payments (eChecks), credit & debit cards, and checks. We encourage families to use ACH payments, which draft directly from your checking or savings account for convenience and efficiency, and charge a 2.8% surcharge for all credit card payments. **Please ensure timely payments to secure your camper’s spot and avoid any inconvenience.**

*The deposit is part of the total tuition and is due with your registration application to secure your camper’s spot.*

### Waitlists

Once Summer Camp, Quarter B-4, or Family Camp reaches full capacity, we establish a waitlist in the order of received applications. Joining the waitlist does not require a deposit. For Summer Camp, our waitlists operate per unit per term. In Quarter B-4, separate waitlists are maintained for the Pikas and the Marmots.

We understand that being placed on a waitlist can be disappointing. It is our goal to make this process as easy and straightforward as possible. Know that there is still hope for a great summer and that we give our waitlist thoughtful attention. Between now and the camp’s start, enrollment statuses tend to change as families adjust their summer plans. While it’s impossible to predict the exact number of open spaces for a given unit or term, historically, cancellations have been infrequent for Quarter B-4 and Family Camp. When spots become available, and we can move a camper off the waitlist, we promptly notify eligible campers via email and phone, using the information provided in your CampBrain waitlist application.

*Regrettably,* *we are unable to increase our capacity due to limitations, such as occupancy restrictions, dining hall capacities, staffing considerations, and program logistics.* *We deeply appreciate your understanding of these constraints. We genuinely wish we could accommodate every interested camper, and we thank you for considering Cheley Colorado Camps*!

*We have developed these [Community Agreements](https://www.cheley.com/required-skills-for-camp-success/) to educate all campers and their families on the essential criteria necessary to participate in our programs. Please read carefully!*

## FAQs

### Do I need to join a waitlist years in advance to secure a spot at camp?

Nope! Unlike some other camps where you might need to join a waitlist years in advance, we keep it simple. Registration for the following summer typically opens on **October 1st**. Just enroll your camper when registration opens. We only start a waitlist after a unit reaches capacity—until then, it’s open enrollment!

### Do you carry over the waitlist from year to year?

We start fresh each summer! Our waitlist does not carry over from year to year. This means you do not need to add your child/camper to a waitlist years ahead of time to enroll. However, if you were on the waitlist the previous year, you’ll receive earlier access to enrollment for the following summer.

### Do you offer financial assistance?

Yes! Our primary partner for financial assistance is **A Thousand Summers** (formerly the John Austin Cheley Foundation). Since its founding in 1989, [A Thousand Summers](https://athousandsummers.org/) has provided over 1,900 camperships—valued at more than $9 million—to help deserving youth attend Cheley and other partner camps.

Camperships are designed for campers from families for whom a high-quality residential camp experience would otherwise be out of reach. Eligibility for Full Support or Partial Support is determined by the JACF Income Eligibility and Family Contribution Table.

**Important:**
A Thousand Summers begins reviewing applications each fall and **typically completes its selection process by December 1.** We strongly encourage interested families to apply early.

A Thousand Summers also partners with mentors, schools, and youth-serving nonprofits to guide families through the application process, help campers prepare for camp, complete Capstone Service Projects, and stay connected after the summer experience.

#### What if we miss the ATS deadline or don’t quite meet their eligibility?

While A Thousand Summers is the best avenue for full camperships, we understand that every financial situation is unique. In some years, thanks to generous alumni and friends of Cheley, we may be able to consider limited additional assistance. In those cases, families are welcome to reach out to us directly and share what level of support might make camp possible. We can’t guarantee availability, but we’re always happy to have the conversation!

### How can I support financial assistance for campers?

You can help carry forward Jack Cheley’s vision by supporting the **Jack Cheley Endowed Campership**, ensuring that—every year—a deserving camper experiences Cheley in his name. To donate to A Thousand Summers, please visit [Sponsor a Camper](https://www.cheley.com/sponsor-a-camper/).

At times, thanks to generous alumni and friends of Cheley, we are able to provide partial assistance directly. If you are interested in supporting a camper through Cheley, we welcome those conversations! Please [reach out](https://www.cheley.com/contact-us/).

### Do you recommend summer camp trip insurance?

If you feel that there is even a remote possibility that you will need to cancel your camper’s enrollment, we suggest you purchase Cancellation Insurance. We recommend the A+ Program Protection through [TravMark](https://travmark.com/insurance-products/a-plus-program-insurance/).

If you feel there is a significant chance of needing to cancel enrollment, especially after May 1, this program is only available to purchase during the enrollment process (or within 20 days after initial payment). Please evaluate this “Cancel For Any Reason” insurance plan carefully and determine if it is a good fit for your circumstances. When using the “Cancel For Any Reason” benefit, the plan pays out 65% of tuition.

### Are there any extra costs?

We strive for transparency regarding fees and expenses to help families plan and budget accordingly. Our tuition covers everything needed for a complete camp experience, with only a few additional costs that may apply. Here’s a detailed breakdown:

**Included in Tuition:**

- All activities and program offerings
- Housing and lodging
- Food and snacks (excluding treats)
- Weekly laundry service
- All necessary equipment (everything required but not on the packing list)
- Blue Kerchief for each camper
- Any patches or recognitions received
- End of summer yearbook
- On-site medical care

**Additional Costs (if applicable):**

- **Personal Expenses:** Includes personal items, merchandise, treats, and other store purchases. Costs vary based on individual spending.
- **Credits for One-Way Emails:** Families may purchase credits to send emails to campers through our system.
- **Facial Recognition for Photos:** Families can choose to add this optional service for photos.
- **Sleeping Bag/Pad Rental:** $35/$15
- **Bus/Shuttle Transportation:** $45 per way between Denver International Airport and Cheley on Arrival/Departure Days. A $175 fee applies for special trips to/from the airport, covering fuel and staff time.
- **Airport Spending Money:** $15 provided for spending if campers go to the airport.
- **Additional Variable Fees:**
  - Prescription/Medication
  - Unaccompanied Minor (UM) Fees
  - Luggage Fees
  - Lost and Found Shipping Costs (if items are found and need to be shipped back)

For any specific questions about these fees or to get more details, please contact our office. We’re here to assist and ensure a smooth camp experience for your camper!

### Is there a camp store?

Yes! The Camp Store is open each morning for about 45 minutes so campers can grab any last-minute supplies or equipment needed for that day’s program. In the afternoon, it’s open for an hour and a half—after program and rest hour—for “treats” and shopping. During this time, campers can purchase snacks, clothing, gear, personal care items, and fun Cheley merch. Campers in Lower Chipeta, Lower Ski Hi, and all Quarter B-4 campers may purchase one treat per day ($1.50 or less), while campers in other units may purchase two.  On average, campers spend $125 to $175 per term during Summer Camp, and $20 to $50 during Quarter B-4.

The camp store does not accept cash. During the enrollment process in the Store Form, you will have the option to add a Camp Store “Deposit.” Put simply, **this lets us know how much your camper can spend at the Camp Store.** This deposit is not paid up front – you will only be charged for your camper’s purchases at the end of the summer (i.e., what they actually spend).

When campers visit the Camp Store, they can ask the Camp Store Manager what their remaining balance is – we call this their “Camp Store Wallet.” If at anytime during the summer your camper’s Camp Store Wallet reaches $25, we will contact you to see if you would like to raise their spending limit. You can also “add money” or request access to your camper’s Camp Store Wallet, including their balance and purchases, at any time by contacting us.

Again, **you will only be charged for your camper’s purchases, not their entire deposit.** At the end of each Summer Camp term during Final Saturday and on Departure Day for Quarter B-4, families may visit the camp store at Land O’Peaks and the TEs or browse online – credit card, Apple Pay, Google Pay, Cash App, and Afterpay are accepted at both.

The Camp Store provides a great opportunity for campers to learn responsibility and work on decision-making skills. **We highly recommend you sit down with your camper(s) to discuss the Camp Store and using credit**–so that they know their spending limit and what they should be spending their credit on. A conversation about budgeting and only getting what is needed can provide a valuable lesson.
