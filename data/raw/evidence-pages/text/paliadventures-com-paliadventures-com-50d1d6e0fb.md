## Summer Camp Should be Awesome

Set high in the San Bernardino Mountains, just 90 minutes East of Los Angeles, Pali Adventures is a one-of-a-kind overnight summer camp where kids feel known, try things they never thought they could, and come home proud of who they are becoming. Campers shape their own days by choosing morning specialties and afternoon electives that match their interests, creating an experience that feels personal from the start.

Each day is thoughtfully structured to encourage growth. Campers are supported by staff who know them by name, challenged in ways that feel exciting and achievable, and surrounded by a community that makes trying something new feel safe. The result is a summer filled with confidence-building moments, real friendships, and experiences that stay with them long after camp ends.

## Campers

We welcome campers ages **6 – 16**, offering age-appropriate programs that balance independence with the right level of guidance. **Juniors (8–12)** enjoy a nurturing environment where counselors help them build confidence, try new activities, and make friends with ease. **Seniors (13–16)** get more freedom to design their own schedule, dive deeper into their chosen specialties, and take on leadership roles around camp.

For our younger explorers, we also offer **mini camp**, designed for campers age 6-8 who are ready to take their first step into the world of overnight camp and are excited for a shorter, gently paced introduction.

While Juniors and Seniors have separate cabins and activity groups to ensure comfort and safety, the camp itself is **co-ed and community-focused** – bringing everyone together for camp-wide games, evening events, and unforgettable shared adventures.

## Staff & Safety

![Safety Image](https://paliadventures.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Specialties_Img_6.jpg)

Safety is not an afterthought at Pali Adventures. It is built into every part of the experience. From staffing and training to daily routines and emergency preparedness, care and oversight are constant.

Accreditation through the American Camp Association reflects a commitment to high standards across all areas of camp life. An average staff age of 23, and extensive training create an environment where campers are supported, supervised, and free to explore with confidence.

Staff members are trained in first aid, CPR, emergency response, and homesickness support. Clear procedures and consistent presence help campers feel secure while still giving them the space to grow, try new things, and enjoy their independence.

## Accreditations

High standards are the foundation of everything that happens here. Accreditation is not symbolic. It reflects disciplined systems, consistent supervision, and a commitment to operating at a level families can trust.
Accreditation through the American Camp Association and the Western Association of Independent Camps requires meeting 250+ independently reviewed standards across staffing, training, healthcare, safety, programming, and daily operations. These standards are designed to protect campers while supporting an environment where growth, confidence, and independence can happen safely.

Participation in these accreditation programs is voluntary and requires continued compliance and recurring evaluation. It reflects our commitment to accountability, continuous improvement, and delivering an experience that meets the highest expectations for care, quality, and consistency.

![Accreditation Image](https://paliadventures.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Specialties_Img_7.jpg)

![Left Logo](https://paliadventures.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Specialties_Img_9.png)

The Western Association of Independent Camps (WAIC) is a professional organization of camps in the US that are dedicated to providing campers and staff excellent camp experiences and personal growth opportunities in a caring, fun, and friendly environment.

Each WAIC camp is unique in its location, program, and culture. All WAIC camps are accredited by the American Camp Association (ACA) and maintain best practices in the camp industry.

![Right Logo](https://paliadventures.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Specialties_Img_10.png)

Accreditation educates camp owners and directors in administering key aspects of camp operation, particularly those related to program quality and the health and safety of campers and staff.

The camp’s voluntary participation in the ACA’s professional development and peer-review accreditation process demonstrates a commitment to providing the best possible camp experience.

## Specialties

[ALL SPECIALTIES](https://paliadventures.com/camp-programs/specialties/)

Our 27 specialties form the core of each camper’s experience. These are not drop-in activities or one-off classes. Specialties are weeklong focus tracks where campers spend three hours each morning building skills in an area they are excited to learn. Guided by certified, expert instructors, campers train consistently throughout the week and see real progress by the end of their session.

Whether flying on a trapeze, performing on stage, creating art, or pushing physical limits, campers are supported by instructors who set clear expectations and encourage progress. The focus is not just on what campers try, but on what they gain. Confidence, resilience, pride, and a sense of accomplishment that grows with each day.
