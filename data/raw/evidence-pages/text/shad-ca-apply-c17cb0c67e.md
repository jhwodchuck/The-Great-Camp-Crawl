One Summer,
Endless Impact

Shad isn’t just a summer program. It’s a life-changing experience that inspires students to explore their passions, build leadership, and connect with a community of dreamers and doers. Through hands-on STEAM learning, real-world challenges, and lifelong friendships, Shad empowers next generation of leaders to see what’s possible and gives them the confidence to make it happen.

Your Shad
Your Way

Shad offers four unique ways to experience the program. Each pathway follows the same balanced design of STEAM, community, and design challenges, with enriching experiences that build confidence and leadership.

All on-campus programs ([Live-in](https://www.shad.ca/shad-programs/live-in/), [A-Plus](https://www.shad.ca/shad-programs/a-plus/), and [Unplugged](https://www.shad.ca/shad-programs/unplugged/)) are offered at the same fee, while [Shad Virtual](https://www.shad.ca/shad-programs/virtual/) offers a flexible online option.

## Details

2026 Program Dates

**Live-in / A-Plus / Unplugged:** July 5 – July 31 | 24/7
**Virtual:** July 6 – July 31 | Monday – Friday, 11 am – 5 pm ET

Who can apply?

We’re looking for [**Grade 10 and 11 students**](https://www.shad.ca/participate/#who-can-apply)who are curious, creative, and ready to make a difference.

Fees & Assistance

At Shad Canada, we believe that cost should never be a barrier to potential. Go to [**Fees & Assistance**](https://www.shad.ca/participate/#fees-and-financial-support) page to learn more about the program cost, or explore our [**scholarship**](https://www.shad.ca/scholarships/) options.

Accessibility

To request application accommodations such as needing application material in an alternate format for students with disabilities or students in remote areas please contact **[Michelle@shad.ca](mailto:Michelle@shad.ca)**.

Indigenous Support

Shad is committed to welcoming and supporting First Nations, Inuit, and Métis students in our programs. Visit our [Indigenous](https://www.shad.ca/participate/indigenous/) Page to learn about dedicated supports, funding opportunities, and stories from Indigenous Shad students.
