## How Much Does a Camp Cost in 2025, and What Packages Are Available?

[Camp](https://www.logiscool.com/en-de/programs/camps) prices in 2025 vary greatly depending on the theme, location, duration, and services included. Daily rates typically range from the equivalent of about 25 to 55 EUR, while a full camp week can cost anywhere between 130 and 390 EUR. Whether your budget is closer to 170 EUR or 250 EUR, it’s possible to find the perfect camp if you know what to look for.

At first glance, these prices may seem high. But when you consider that they usually include multiple daily activities, meals, and qualified supervision, the value becomes clear — especially when camps also provide lasting memories and skill development.

Many organizers offer flexible packages that can include meals, full-day programs, access to tools and materials, and, in the case of residential camps, accommodation and round-the-clock care. There are also half-day options at lower costs, and discounts are often available for siblings or multiple sessions.

## Full-Day, Half-Day, or Residential Camps – Which Is Right for Your Child?

The type of camp you choose depends largely on your child’s age, maturity, and your family’s daily routine. **Day camps** are a great fit for children between the ages of 6 and 14, especially for those who aren’t quite ready to spend nights away from home. These camps typically run from morning to late afternoon and are often hosted at local schools, digital studios, or community centers — depending on the region.

**Half-day camps**, usually taking place in the morning hours, are ideal for older, more independent kids. These camps often focus on a specific theme — such as game design, artificial intelligence, or animation — allowing participants to explore a topic in depth while leaving the rest of the day open for other plans or family time.

**Residential camps** cater to kids aged 8 and above who are ready for a sleepaway adventure. These camps provide a more immersive experience, offering deeper community bonding and more intensive, theme-based programs in a setting away from home.

## What Types of Camps Can You Choose From?

Today’s parents can choose from a wide variety of themed camps — from traditional sports camps to creative, scientific, or digital programs. Since every child is different, the best choice depends on their personal interests.

- **Sports camps:** Perfect for active kids, with games, team challenges, and adventure activities.
- **Creative camps:** Drawing, music, crafts, and self-expression for artistic children using a range of tools and materials.
- **Skill-building camps:** Develop logical thinking, motor skills, and creativity through playful and engaging activities.
- **Adventure and nature camps:** Hiking, wildlife observation, and outdoor survival experiences.
- **STEM and science camps:** Fun experiments, logic puzzles, and discoveries for curious minds using digital tools.
- **[Digital camps:](https://www.logiscool.com/en-de/programs/camps)** Coding, game development, and animation — for example, through programs like those offered by [Logiscool](https://www.logiscool.com/en-de).

## How to Choose the Right Camp?

Want your child to have a fun and safe summer? Consider [these factors](https://www.logiscool.com/en-de/blog/education/7-tips-to-choose-the-right-camp-for-your-child) when picking the right camp:

- **Interests:** Choose a camp that aligns with your child’s passions — whether it’s sports, creativity, nature, or tech.
- **Camp type:** Does your child want to sleep away or come home daily? Residential and day camps offer different experiences.
- **Accessibility:** Are there camps near your location? This is especially important for day programs.
- **Environment:** The camp should be safe, well-equipped, and inspiring — especially for indoor or tech-based activities.
- **Budget:** Think about how much you’re willing to spend. Most camps offer a variety of packages to fit different budgets.
- **Staff experience:** Qualified and enthusiastic camp leaders are key to a safe and enjoyable experience.

If you choose thoughtfully, not only will your child have an unforgettable summer — but you’ll also enjoy peace of mind.
