# Yellowstone Horse Mini

A mini version of our beloved Yellowstone Horse Summer Camp has arrived!

Adventure loving, horseback riding, outdoor enthusiasts will saddle up and get ready to live like cowgirls in the wild west of Montana. This 8-day outdoor adventure summer camp for teens is action packed!

The heart of this Alpengirl adventure is a 3-day backcountry horse packing trip that will have us trotting through the rugged beauty of the Northwest Rockies, where we’ll learn the necessary skills to take care of pack animals in the wilderness, enjoy the spoils of a sprawling landscape, and end our days around the campfire together.

Horsepacking might be the focus of this trip, but we’ll also embark on incredible Big Sky adventures like whitewater rafting, hot spring soaking, and river floating.

Lace up your boots and get ready to join us–adventure awaits!

## Trip Highlights

- 3-day horse pack adventure in the Centennial Mountains
- Float the Madison River
- Raft the Gallatin River
- Swimming in alpine lakes and rivers
- Soaking in thermal hot springs

- This trip starts and departs from Bozeman, Montana

Yellowstone Horse Mini

**Day 1**/8

**Welcome to Alpengirl!**

 Arriving at Bozeman-Yellowstone International Airport, your Alpenguides will greet you and introduce you to the rest of your camp group. Name games and icebreakers abound before we all hop into the van and drive to our first campsite. Once we learn how to set up our tents, we’ll continue to get to know each other, then enjoy the first of many delicious and fresh camp meals – perhaps build your own burritos with veggies, rice and all the fixin's served with organic blue corn chips and salsa!

![](https://www.alpengirlcamp.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/Yellowstone-horse-adds-15-scaled-1-768x1024.webp) ![](https://www.alpengirlcamp.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/horse-arms-ups-768x512.avif)

**Float on!**

 Waking up well rested, you’ll be amazed at how massive the blue sky seems as you step out of your tent. Bring your sleeping pad out to the yoga circle and allow your Alpenyogi to guide you through relaxing stretches as your body wakes up. A delicious breakfast awaits as we get changed and learn a little more about our upcoming days ahead and of all the nuts and bolts of spending time outdoors.

 This afternoon, Alpenguides will get everyone situated in their inflatable rafts and kayaks, and go over paddling techniques and all things safety. We’ll spend the afternoon having fun swimming, splashing and laughing as we enjoy a scenic day's float down the Madison River with new friends!

![](https://www.alpengirlcamp.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/Emma-eat-768x1025.avif) ![](https://www.alpengirlcamp.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/Yellowstone-horse-adds-14-768x576.webp)

**Saddle up, cowgirls!**

 Today we’ll pack up camp and make our way to the Centennial Valley and meet our experienced horse guides. Our horse outfitters will welcome us to this remote and pristine wilderness area where we’ll learn the ins and outs of horse packing. Girls will be assigned their horses, learn how to saddle and bridle them, and how to pack and securely load all of our gear onto a pack mule for our multi-day expedition into the mountains.

 Over the next 3 days, girls will learn basic western trail horsemanship skills at a beginner level. We will have the chance to ride through wildflower fields, across crystal clear, spring-fed creeks, and over rugged and steep mountain passes. Along with our daily rides, we may also have a chance to take the horses out for a sunset stroll, try out riding bareback, and even learn to use a cowboy lariat rope for lassoing! We can stay fresh and clean on the expedition by having an Alpenolympic Bucket Hair Washing competition.

 Our backcountry camps are always in amazing locations with incredible views. One night, we’ll use the beautiful backdrop of the mountains to stage our very own Alpenolympic Talent Show, and we’ll join our hosts around the campfire for dinner, learning about the early history and people of the area, and singing camp songs together. Laughing with one another while watching the sun set behind the Centennial Mountains as we watch our horses wander and graze in the grassy meadows is sure to be a memory Alpengirls will never forget!

![](https://www.alpengirlcamp.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/horse-new-4-768x1024.avif) ![](https://www.alpengirlcamp.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/Yellowstone-horse-adds-19-scaled-1-768x1024.webp)

**Let’s go rafting!**

 We’ll trade in our saddles for paddles today, when we will enjoy our much anticipated, rip-roarin’ rafting adventure on the Gallatin River! We’ll have an afternoon of incredible whitewater rafting through Class II-IV rapids, with plenty of opportunities for splashing, swimming, and laughing. Fans of the movie “A River Runs Through It” will notice that the scenery looks familiar, as the Gallatin is where much of the movie was filmed.

 We’ll end our day relaxing in our campsite’s mineral hot springs to rest our tired bodies, and enjoy another evening of delicious food (s’mores please!) and one another’s company at camp.

![](https://www.alpengirlcamp.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/Yellowstone-horse-adds-22-768x576.webp) ![](https://www.alpengirlcamp.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/Cascades-Coast-25-768x512.webp)

**Bozeman exploration**

 After a well-deserved lie-in and leisurely breakfast, we'll head to downtown Bozeman to explore, shop, and maybe even stop in to a local ice cream stand!

 Our last day concludes with a special dinner to kick off our End of Session Awards ceremony, in which Alpenguides present each camper with a unique award celebrating their contribution to our group! Skill beads and necklaces will be awarded as beautiful keepsakes to recognize the hard work each camper has done to commit to learning new outdoor living skills, all while sharing laughter, hugs, tears, and talk of when we’ll see each other next.

![](https://www.alpengirlcamp.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/award2-768x1024.avif) ![](https://www.alpengirlcamp.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/53881007686_0de08605c0_o-768x576.avif)

**It’s time to go home!**

 Exchanging hugs with your friends, you’ll depart from Bozeman-Yellowstone International Airport, knowing that you’ve had a ton of fun and accomplished great things as an Alpengirl and will be a part of our fantastic community for life. See you next summer!

## Required Horse Riding Experience:

A limited-to-considerable amount of horseback trail riding experience is required to attend this session.

Horses are chosen to suit each girl’s comfort level, size, and riding ability, and campers will be introduced to their horses prior to departure.

Due to densely forested and sometimes steep, rugged, and uneven terrain, cantering is often not available to riders. Backcountry conditions offer obstacles like downed trees, remaining patches of snow fields, or running creeks.

Campers may advance in their riding skills once those skills are adequately demonstrated to guides and only if terrain allows for it. On the right terrain, girls that can walk a horse properly can go to a trot, then to a canter, etc.

Campers with limited horse experience:

Evaluating personal comfort level and confidence prior to camp is required. Girls should not be afraid of horses and should have at least one full day of western trail horseback riding experience.

Campers with considerable horse experience:

Call before registering to assess abilities, desires, and interests for more experienced riders. Girls should be aware that this is a multi-adventure itinerary that offers many other non-horse related activities.

## Campsite Amenities

### Five nights in “Front country” Campgrounds:

Five nights are spent at a private “front country” pre-assigned campsites with running potable water, and a private outhouse or flushing toilet.

### Two nights “Backcountry” Camping:

Two nights are spent in tents in wilderness “backcountry” areas where we will pack in our water and pack out our waste and learn and practice basic Leave No Trace camping skills such as how to select a campsite, how to go to the bathroom, and how to keep clean outdoors.

## Why Choose This Trip

Campers who love horses but also want to go rafting, swimming, river floating and explore Big Sky Country will love this camp.

There are adventures around every bend with the added bonus of caring for your very own horse in a wilderness setting! From rivers, to lakes, to mountains, this mini version of our horse trips meets the high demand for busy campers to squeeze in an incredible experience in a variety of outdoor settings in which they can fall in love with the natural world! Limited to 12 campers in the group.
