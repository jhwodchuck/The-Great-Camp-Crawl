The California Governor's Mansion is very pretty, but oddly unimpressive in person. It's in a dubious neighborhood, it's on a comparatively small lot and is right on the street, there's a cheap motel across the street (where some past governors would use the pool), and it isn't the most impressive building around. If you go a few block and cross 16th street, you're among the gorgeous Victorians and Painted Ladies of Midtown. Just a few blocks away, there are houses that are both larger and more beautiful, and which are shaded by an urban forest of huge old trees.

I lived in Midtown for years, and when I moved in I was shocked to find that "Painted Lady" houses weren't limited to San Francisco.
