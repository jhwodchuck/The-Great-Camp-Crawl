## Join Shad

Who can apply?

Shad welcomes high school students in Grades 10 and 11 (Secondary IV and V in Québec) who are curious about the world and eager to take on new challenges. We’re looking for students who are driven, creative, and community-minded. If you ask big questions, embrace diverse perspectives, and want to make a difference in STEAM fields, you already have what it takes to become a Shad.

With a limited number of spots available every year, there is a selection process in place to determine participants. Our selection process examines more than academics. We look for potential: the spark of leadership, a willingness to collaborate, and the courage to think differently.

Shad is committed to making our program financially accessible to students. Fee assistance is based on annual household income and is available to domestic applicants only.

- If your annual (total gross) household income is under $40,000, your program fee is fully covered.
- If your annual (total gross) household income is between $40,000 and $150,000, your program fee will be reduced on a sliding scale.
- If your annual (total gross) household income is over $150,000, you may still qualify for fee assistance in certain circumstances.

Finance Your Shad

At Shad Canada, we believe that cost should never be a barrier to potential. Each year, with the support of our generous donors and partners, we provide up to $5 million in [**scholarships**](https://www.shad.ca/scholarships/) and fee assistance, ensuring that talented students from every background can access this life-changing experience. In addition to bursaries and scholarships offered through Shad, students can also seek support from their communities, schools, or private sponsors. Many Shads have secured funding this way, with their experiences standing as proof of their leadership and resilience.

## Campuses

Pathways to post-secondary

Each summer, Shad partners with leading post-secondary institutions across Canada to deliver immersive programs that allow students to experience campus life firsthand. From living in residence and exploring state-of-the-art facilities to engaging with professors and discovering the unique character of different cities, every Shad campus offers a window into the future.

When accepted, students are asked to list their preferred campuses. While these preferences are carefully considered, final placements are made by Shad to ensure a truly pan-Canadian experience. Regardless of placement, every Shad program delivers the same high standard of learning, community, and impact.

## FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Below are various common questions that come up about Shad and our programs:

Program / General

What Shad programs are running in 2026?

Shad offers a suite of immersive [programs](https://www.shad.ca/shad-programs/) designed to ignite curiosity, build confidence, and prepare students in grades 10 and 11 to thrive in a fast-changing world.

[Live-in](https://www.shad.ca/shad-programs/#live-in)
[Virtual](https://www.shad.ca/shad-programs/#virtual)



What are the dates for the Shad2026 program?

**Live-in / A-Plus / Unplugged:** July 5 – July 31 | 24/7
**Virtual:** July 6 – July 31 | Monday – Friday, 11 am – 5 pm ET



What campuses offer the Shad2026 program?

Shad’s Live-in program is hosted at our carefully selected post-secondary [campuses](https://www.shad.ca/participate/#campuses) and Shad’s Virtual program is powered by Athabasca University.

Learn more about [Shad’s Live-in experience](https://www.shad.ca/shad-programs/live-in/) and [Shad’s Virtual experience](https://www.shad.ca/shad-programs/digital/) and our two program options – [Unplugged](https://www.shad.ca/shad-programs/unplugged/) and [A-Plus](https://www.shad.ca/shad-programs/a-plus/).



What campuses will be hosting A-Plus and Unplugged?

The number and locations of Shad campuses offering A-Plus and Unplugged will be determined by the demand from accepted applicants.



Do I get to choose which campus I go to?

While we take into consideration your preferred campus options, Shad ultimately chooses your campus in order to promote a pan-Canadian experience for all participants.



Who delivers the campus programs?

[Well-qualified](https://www.shad.ca/campuses/)team members work hard to ensure that all aspects of the experience – academic, logistical and social – meet or exceed expectations.

Many of these dedicated team members are top-rated post-secondary professors. Volunteers from all areas of the university and business community also add to the mix to create a dynamic experience.  All team members and volunteers subscribe to the highest code of conduct as defined and maintained by Shad Canada.



Can students leave the Shad Live-in program to attend another event?

No. Shad participants must commit to attending the entire duration of the program.



What should I do if I require accommodation under Human Rights legislation?

Students who require accommodations related to disabilities, cultural/religious practices, or any other human rights protected ground should contact Shad Canada as early as possible to discuss their accommodation needs and to ensure Shad Canada can meet their needs. Careful consideration should be made prior to accepting the offer to participate.

Requests can be sent to [**info@shad.ca**](mailto:info@shad.ca)



Can I receive high school or post-secondary credit for participating in Shad?

Shad can count towards [high school credit](https://www.shad.ca/credits/) for participants from Saskatchewan, Manitoba, Newfoundland and Labrador, Prince Edward Island, and Nova Scotia. The Shad experience can also qualify as a Sector-Partnered Experience for SHSM students in Ontario and towards CAS requirements for IB diploma students. Shad’s Virtual campus participants also have the opportunity to complete credentials from Athabasca University.

Fees

How much does the Shad2026 program cost?

Learn more about Shad’s program [fee](https://www.shad.ca/participate/#fees-and-financial-support), [financial assistance](https://www.shad.ca/participate/#fees-and-financial-support), [scholarships](https://www.shad.ca/scholarships/) and more.



Is financial assistance available to assist with program fees?

Yes! Shad offers financial support to cover full and partial program fees. Every year we provide over $5 Million in financial assistance for families. Every application includes a parent contact section so they can consider financial assistance opportunities.

Visit our [scholarship](https://www.shad.ca/scholarships/) page for additional opportunities as well.



Do I pay the program fee all at once?

No. When an offer is made, applicants submit a $750 deposit to secure their spot in the program.  The remaining balance is made in two separate instalments.



Is the Shad program fee tax deductible?

The Shad program fee is NOT tax deductible.

Applications

Who can apply?

High School students currently completing Grade 10, 11 (Secondaire IV, V in Quebec).
[We are looking for students](https://www.shad.ca/participate/#about-shad) who are community-minded, creative, engaged, curious and want to make an impact in Canada and the world.



When is the application due?

The deadline is January 5, 2026 at 11:59 p.m. ET.



How do you apply to the Shad program?

Students complete an [online application](https://www.shad.ca/apply).

Note:  Admission into the Shad program is competitive, therefore applicants need to put their best effort forward when completing the application.

To request application accommodations such as needing application material in an alternate format for students with disabilities or students in remote areas please contact [Michelle@shad.ca](mailto:Michelle@shad.ca)



What types of things are asked on the Application Form?

Applicants complete a profile, answer questions, upload their most recent report card, and provide a reference contact.

For more application details, **[click here](https://www.shad.ca/application-summary/)**.

For more reference details, **[click here](https://www.shad.ca/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Shad2026_Reference_Outline.pdf)**.



What criteria is considered in the selection process?

Applicants are assessed on several factors highlighting their unique talents and contributions. Factors such as overall excellence, extracurricular involvement (i.e. volunteerism, part-time work, family care responsibilities), creativity, reflection and commitment in making the world a better place.



What is the minimum academic average that is needed to apply?

Applicants should demonstrate academic excellence but there is no “cut-off’ average for applying.



Does a school need to nominate applicants?

No, we do not require a school nomination for a student to be eligible to apply.



When will students know if they have been selected to attend the program?

All applicants will be informed of their status (accepted, declined) in February 2026. Decisions around financial assistance will be communicated at the same time.



Any other questions?

For questions not covered here, please contact [info@shad.ca](mailto:info@shad.ca) for your request.
