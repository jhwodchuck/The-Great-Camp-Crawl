## Designed for Children, Built for Success

## From Digital Skills Development to Python Diploma

Our curriculum provides a structured path from **digital literacy** to **advanced programming**, **game design**, and **AI**. Unlike many coding schools, it’s designed to match kids' learning pace, ensuring steady and effective progress.

## Learning by Doing: The Key to Mastery

![](//assets.logiscool.com/f/57188/708x399/67c5cd3c00/mylogiscool_01.jpg)

At Logiscool, theory and practice go hand in hand. Research confirms that blending theory with practical projects leads to better retention and a stronger foundation for future success.

**That’s why in every lesson:**

✔ Kids create **cool projects** while learning the underlying concepts

✔ They get immediate **hands-on experience**, making it easier to understand and apply new skills

✔ Practical learning boosts **confidence**, **creativity**, and **problem-solving** abilities

With this method, digital skills become second nature, helping kids build a strong foundation for future studies and careers.

***Why Learning by Doing Works:
Research shows that active learning reduces failure rates by 34% and improves student performance by nearly 50% (Harvard Gazette, 2019).***

### 2. Scoolcode – Our Unique Coding Platform

Our unique educational platform is **developed for easy transition** from visual coding to text based programming languages.

![blox coding icon](//assets.logiscool.com/f/57188/719x496/c46d923bbe/icon_blox.png)

### Blox coding Level 1-2

We start coding education with “building blocks”. Our unique visual coding method is always best adjusted to all ages and knowledge levels.

![mix coding icon](//assets.logiscool.com/f/57188/720x497/a03c619a71/icon_mix_v3.png)

### Mix coding Level 3

As a second step, students start writing codes in MIX mode, where they can see both the building blox and the text-based languages in parallel.

![text coding icon](//assets.logiscool.com/f/57188/720x497/efd7d2cc40/icon_text_v3.png)

### Text coding Level 4

Once they feel confident to leave the visual blocks behind, they can move to text-based programming languages, like Python and C# through different game developer engines like Unity and Godot.
