# SAMS Student Life Information

This site is intended for students, and families, who are enrolled in the Summer Academy for Math and Science (SAMS) Program. Please visit the [Office of Admission SAMS website](https://admission.enrollment.cmu.edu/pages/access-sams "Office of Admissions SAMS website") for information about applying to the program and the logistics of enrollment. The Center for Student Diversity and Inclusion oversees the implementation of the SAMS program for students after they are enrolled.

## The Student Experience

The SAMS program aims to develop students both inside and outside of the classroom. The program is designed to mirror the first semester of the CMU freshman experience including orientation, rigorous academics, and experiential learning all in a safe and supportive environment.

# Staff Information

The SAMS Student Life experience is supervised by [Bianca DeJesus](../../about-us/who-we-are/index.html "Bianca's contact info") out of the Center for Student Diversity and Inclusion. Bianca asupervises the Program Coordinators and Resident Assistants.

## Program Coordinators (PC)

The PCs are part of the professional staff for the SAMS program. Program Coordinators hold bachelors degrees as well as bring a wealth of knowledge and understanding to the SAMS residential life component. These individuals are responsible for the supervision of the RA staff and the day to day operation of the SAMS residential life experience.

## Resident Assistant (RA)

SAMS RAs are student staff members who facilitate the co-curricular experience and plan group activities for students in the program. RAs have the most direct out-of-classroom contact with SAMS students, each being responsible for 10-15 residential students.

# SAMS 2018 FAQs

As you prepare for SAMS, here are some frequently asked questions typically posed by incoming students and families. All further details about the program will be covered in the SAMS Handbook (students will get access to the handbook after they deposit and complete the enrollment process). If you have a question that is not addressed below, please email [cmu-sams@andrew.cmu.edu](mailto:cmu-sams@andrew.cmu.edu).

## Am I required to live on campus?

All students are required to live on campus. A part of the Summer Academy for Math and Science experience is learning to live in the residence hall with a roommate.

## What does a typical day look like?

A typical day of the program starts around 7:30am with breakfast and runs through the evening.  The day includes classes, seminars, projects, time to meet with faculty in office hours, as well as breaks for lunch and dinner.  The full details of the student schedule will be outlined in the handbook; this will be distributed to students after they deposit and complete the enrollment process.

## Who will supervise the students in the residence hall?

The residence hall staff consists of two adults (1 male, 1 female) with counseling and residence hall supervision experience. There will also be a team of college student resident assistants who will mentor, advise and support the health, comfort and general welfare of the students. The residence hall staff will live in the residence halls and are available to respond 24 hours a day.

## Are students permitted to leave campus during the program?

In order to build a strong residential community we strongly encourage students to limit weekends away from campus. However, if students must be away overnight or for the weekend, an ["Absence from Campus Release Form"](SAMS Absence from Campus Release Agreement Form.pdf "absence from campus form") signed by the parent or guardian, granting permission for the absence, must be completed. Overnight absences during the week when classes and projects are in session will not be permitted.

## When should I plan to arrive and depart?

Registration and Opening Day are Saturday, June 30.  Participants should plan to arrive and check in between 8:00 a.m. and 1:30 p.m. Students should plan to leave after 5:00 p.m. on Friday, August 10, but before 12:00 p.m. on Saturday, August 11.

## Can I select my roommate?

No, roommates assignments will be made based on information provided on the personal profile form provided in your on-line enrollment packet. These assignments will not be given out prior to arrival.

## Will my child need a laptop computer to participate in SAMS?

If you have a laptop, we encourage you to bring it. While having a personal laptop is convenient, the university has extensive computer labs or clusters available campus-wide. They offer a variety of Windows, Mac and Linux computers, peripherals and an extensive complement of software.

## Can my child arrive early?

Unfortunately, we cannot accommodate early arrivals in the residence hall.

## What are the dining arrangements on campus?

All summer meal plans operate on a debit system, which is encoded on students’ CMU ID cards. No refunds for unused meals are available. The SAMS meal plan, which provides for 19 meals per week (3 meals per day Monday – Friday, 2 meals per day Saturday & Sunday).
