# Quality Bar

Use the smallest output that preserves the important truth.

## Discovery quality bar

A strong discovery candidate has:

- a usable canonical or best-known URL
- basic geography
- a short reason it may qualify
- at least one evidence snippet for overnight status, recent activity, or venue specificity
- explicit uncertainty notes when something is missing

Discovery should favor breadth, but not junk. A short list of plausible candidates is better than a long list of weak guesses.

## Validation quality bar

A candidate should only pass core validation when the evidence supports:

- overnight or residential participation
- recent activity within the last 24 months
- a specific physical venue or session location, or a clearly marked split need

## Enrichment quality bar

A strong enrichment result has:

- official-source evidence
- exact copied wording for key facts when possible
- nulls for unknown values
- notes for ambiguous cases

## Free-tier agent guidance

- Keep outputs compact.
- Avoid long prose explanations.
- Prefer exact snippets over summaries.
- Stop when confidence drops and push the uncertainty into `validation_needs`.
